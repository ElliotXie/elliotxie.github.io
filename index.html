About Me

I'm a Ph.D. student at the University of Wisconsin-Madison specializing in Biostatistics and Bioinformatics. My current work focuses on single-cell analysis, multi-agent large language models (LLMs), and reinforcement learning (RL). I'm passionate about developing computational methods to solve complex biological problems and advance healthcare research.

Research Interests

Single-Cell Analysis

Exploring cellular heterogeneity through advanced computational methods, focusing on identifying cell types and understanding disease mechanisms at the single-cell level.

Multi-Agent LLMs

Developing collaborative language models for complex problem-solving in biomedical research, enabling advanced data analysis and interpretation of scientific literature.

Reinforcement Learning

Applying RL techniques to optimize decision-making processes in healthcare, treatment planning, and drug discovery workflows.
